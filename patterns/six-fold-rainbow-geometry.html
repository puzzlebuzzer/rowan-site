Plain language

This design has six-fold symmetry: the pattern repeats every 60 degrees. I colored neighboring shapes by stepping through a rainbow cycle, so color advances even when the geometry repeats.

Structural description

The underlying geometry consists of repeated units arranged in a hexagonal symmetry group. The coloring rule advances through a cyclic sequence of hues, creating interactions between rotational symmetry and color phase.

What I was experimenting with

I wanted to see how a simple rainbow cycle interacts with a design whose symmetry does not perfectly align with the color period.

Observations

Certain regions begin to form directional color flows even though the geometry itself is rotationally symmetric.
