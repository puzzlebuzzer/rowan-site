Bipartite Flower Tessellation

A repeating floral motif arranged on a bipartite lattice.

Plain language

This pattern repeats the same flower-like shape across the page. The shapes alternate orientation in a way similar to a chessboard: each motif is surrounded by motifs of the opposite type.

Structural description

The tessellation is bipartite: the pattern can be divided into two interleaving sets where no two adjacent tiles belong to the same set. The alternating flower orientations correspond to those two parity classes.

What I was experimenting with

I tried to highlight the two parity classes using warm oranges and reds for one set and cool blues and teals for the other. I also experimented with lighter and darker shades to emphasize vertical columns of motifs.

Observations

Because the motif itself has strong curvature, the eye tends to follow diagonal flows instead of the bipartite structure. The piece became an experiment in how color encoding interacts with geometric repetition.
