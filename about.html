About

Much of my work begins with a simple question:

What kinds of structure emerge when a very small rule is repeated many times?

I explore this through visual experiments, code, and written notes. Many of the pieces on this site start from geometric coloring pages or simple tilings. By applying constrained color sequences, modular cycles, or directional rules, these patterns sometimes reveal unexpected structures.

Some experiments are deliberate attempts to visualize ideas such as symmetry, parity, or modular cycles. Others are explorations of flow, repetition, and interference in a pattern.

Why publish experiments

Most of the pieces here are not finished theories. They are artifacts from an ongoing process of exploration.

  • to document the ideas so they exist outside my head
  • to allow other people to see, interpret, or build on them

A simple artifact can often communicate an idea more clearly than a long explanation.

Multiple ways of understanding

Each piece can be described in several ways:

  • Plain language so anyone can approach the idea
  • Conceptual descriptions for readers interested in systems and structure
  • Formal descriptions for people who prefer mathematical language

None of these perspectives are privileged over the others. They are different lenses on the same underlying idea.

Tools and media

  • geometric coloring and visual studies
  • small software experiments and simulations
  • written notes and essays

Some ideas begin with pencil and markers. Others start in code. Often they move back and forth between the two.

The broader goal

Across all of these explorations, the underlying interest is the same: how simple rules interact with structure, symmetry, and cycles to produce complex behavior.
